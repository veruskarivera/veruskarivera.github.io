ASL To Go


Think about a project idea (no violence) and brainstorm about what features & functions it could have, how technology could be used in new ways, or a better way to do something, a new market or a new user community to serve, an important problem to solve

01

Identifying the Problem

A lot of the Sign Language speakers understand what other people is trying to communicate
them by reading their lips. Due to COVID-19, the population is wearing mask in order to
protect themselves and the rest of the society and this has been a struggle for the ASL
community since everyone's mouth is being covered and they can no longer understand
what people is trying to tell them.

02

Proposing a Solution

Some people started using clear masks in order to try to help the ASL, but this was not 
efficient since these masks are not approved by CDC standards so I started thinking 
and I realized that the best way to help was to design an app that would be able to 
translate from English to American Sign Language and viceversa. After proposing 
the idea to the group we also decided that ASL To Go will also have learning modules
to encourage ASL non-speakers to learn the language.

03

Identifying the User

Since we serve as a learning software for users (American Sign Language  Speakers and Non-American Sign Language Speakers) 16+, it’s important  that we did research into the fundamentals that impact learning new  languages. With that being said, the most significant constraints that ASL  To Go face pertain to linguistic exposure. We found that the earlier in life  that you start to pick up a new language, the higher the chances of mastering it are. As ASL To Go is not geared to teach children, but instead adults, we  face the challenges that come along with language acquisition declining  with age. As designers and content creators, we have to think of innovative  ways to increase learning motivation.

04

Create a Persona

John is working in a local restaurant as a cashier on a busy Saturday night. A girl approaches to him to make an order when he realizes that she is an ASL speaker, they had to struggle to finalize the order but at the end they were able to make it. John wonders if there's a easier way to communicate with a ASL speaker without him requiring to remove his mask.
